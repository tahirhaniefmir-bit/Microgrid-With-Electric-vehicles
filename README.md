# Microgrid-With-Electric-vehicles
Developed a microgrid with electric vehicles to enhance the energy management and sustainability. I have done this project individual basis in the 7th semester and have proven bi-directional flow of power energy from grid to vehicle and vehicle to grid via MATLAB and Simulink.
This project focuses on the development of a microgrid integrated with electric vehicles (EVs) to improve energy management, efficiency, and sustainability. The system is designed to enable bidirectional power flow between the grid and electric vehicles, allowing energy exchange in both directions—Grid-to-Vehicle (G2V) and Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G).

The primary objective of this project is to utilize electric vehicles not only as loads but also as distributed energy storage units within a microgrid. During periods of low demand or excess generation, EVs are charged from the grid (G2V mode). Conversely, during peak demand or power shortages, stored energy in EV batteries is fed back into the grid (V2G mode), thereby supporting grid stability and reducing dependency on conventional energy sources.

The developed system includes a microgrid model consisting of power generation sources, load demand, EV battery storage, and a bidirectional power converter. The bidirectional converter plays a key role in controlling the direction of power flow and maintaining system stability. A control strategy is implemented to manage charging and discharging operations based on grid conditions and energy requirements.

MATLAB and Simulink are used for modeling, simulation, and analysis of the system. The simulation demonstrates the effectiveness of bidirectional power transfer, showing how energy can flow from the grid to the vehicle during charging and from the vehicle back to the grid during discharging. Various operating conditions are tested to evaluate system performance, including changes in load demand and energy availability.

The results confirm that integrating EVs into a microgrid significantly enhances energy management by balancing supply and demand, improving system flexibility, and increasing overall efficiency. Additionally, this approach contributes to sustainability by promoting the use of clean energy and reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

This project was completed individually during the 7th semester, demonstrating strong understanding of power systems, control strategies, and simulation tools. The successful implementation of bidirectional energy flow highlights the potential of EV-integrated microgrids in future smart grid applications.
